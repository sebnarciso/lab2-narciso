My favorite place is Japan!

So far, I was able to visit Tokyo, Osaka, and Kyoto.

Tokyo.

Tokyo was the first city I was able to visit in Japan. The busy streets, the tall buildings, and the swarms of people were all just so exciting to me. I loved seeing the different places the city had to offer. Just wandering the streets was enough to make me feel so excited yet at home.I think another thing I really enjoyed about visiting Tokyo was the fact that everything just seemed so well organized. This was evident in how efficient their trains and public transport was. In addition, I found every person we encountered to be so polite, respectful, and considerate of others.

Gion District, Kyoto.

Gion is Kyoto's most famous geisha district, located around Shijo Avenue between Yasaka Shrine in the east and the Kamo River in the west. It is filled with shops, restaurants and ochaya (teahouses), where geiko (Kyoto dialect for geisha) and maiko (geiko apprentices) entertain.
Visiting Gion really painted the picture of the more cultural side of Japan. What I found so fascinating about the place was the fact that the area was both very culturally-intact, yet also modern and urbanized at the same time.

Navigating Japan.

When I visited Japan, my parents left it up to me to decide on our itenerary which involved planning out each of our days in Japan, researching the different areas, laying out all of the directions, and choosing the hotels we would be staying at. Though it was exhausting, it was completely rewarding once we arrived. On some days, my parents would even let me explore the cities we would visit by myself. The taste of being able to travel and explore on my own was so exciting to me. Hopefully after the pandemic, I get to visit Japan once again.
